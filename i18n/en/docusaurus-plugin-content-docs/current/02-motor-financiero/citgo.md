# Citgo: The Key Piece in the Restructuring

> Citgo Petroleum is the most valuable Venezuelan asset outside Venezuela. Its fate defines the debt restructuring.

## What Citgo Is Today

Citgo Petroleum Corporation is a refinery headquartered in Houston, Texas, with capacity of **~750,000 bpd** across three refineries (Lake Charles, Lemont, Corpus Christi). Indirectly owned by PDVSA through PDV Holding. Since 2019, controlled by a [trust under U.S. court supervision](https://www.reuters.com/business/energy/us-court-appoints-special-master-oversee-citgo-parent-sale-2024-07-15/) following OFAC sanctions.

| Data point | Value | Source |

:::danger Claims exceed asset value
The **USD 19,000+ M in claims** registered in the Delaware court are ~1.5-2x Citgo's estimated value. If a court-ordered sale proceeds, Venezuela loses the asset and still owes money.
:::

## Strategic Options

| Option | Description | Pro | Con |
|--------|-------------|-----|--------|
| **Controlled partial sale** | Sell 30-49% to strategic operator under negotiated terms | Generates immediate liquidity, retains influence | Loses partial operational control |
| **Comprehensive restructuring** | Include Citgo in restructuring package with global haircut | Systemic solution, avoids forced sale | Requires creditor majority agreement |
| **VIN swap** | Exchange Citgo claims for participation in [VIN](/02-motor-financiero/fondo-soberano) | Aligns long-term incentives, protects asset | Creditors may reject illiquid asset |
| **Direct negotiation** | Bilateral agreements with ConocoPhillips and Crystallex | Resolves the two largest claims | High costs, sets precedent |

:::tip Recommendation
The optimal combination is **direct negotiation with ConocoPhillips** (Orinoco Belt JV as partial compensation) + **VIN swap** for PDVSA 2020 bondholders + **partial payment + extension** for Crystallex. This resolves ~USD 13,000 M of the USD 19,000 M in claims without selling the asset.
:::

## Impact on the Total Restructuring

Citgo is not just a refinery — it is the **negotiation currency** for the entire restructuring:

1. **If Citgo is lost** -> Venezuela negotiates from weakness. Creditors know there are no more attachable assets in U.S. jurisdiction.
2. **If Citgo is retained** -> Venezuela can offer guaranteed refining as backing for post-restructuring new bonds.
3. **If Citgo is strategically swapped** -> The largest ICSID claims are resolved with participation in Venezuelan upstream, not cash.
