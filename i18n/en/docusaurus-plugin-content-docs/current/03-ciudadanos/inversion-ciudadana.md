# Universal Citizen Investment: We Are All Shareholders

> Article 1, ICCPR (UN, 1966): "All peoples may freely dispose of their natural wealth and resources." The key word is **peoples**, not States.

## 4 Layers of Participation

1. **Automatic Dividend** — For EVERYONE. No investment required. Model: [Alaska PFD USD 1,000/person (2025)](https://pfd.alaska.gov/)
2. **Citizen Bonds** — Starting at USD 10. Fixed interest + participation
3. **Tech Venezuela Fund** — 20-30% of the fund invests in domestic tech (startups, data centers, AI). National VC model
4. **VIN Shares** — Starting at USD 500. IPO vision years 8-12

## Dividend 2x Reform: Equality from Day 1

:::danger Structural critique: the 2x model creates built-in inequality
Piketty (*Capital in the Twenty-First Century*, 2014) demonstrated that when r > g — when return on capital exceeds economic growth — inequality self-amplifies. Stiglitz (*The Price of Inequality*, 2012) documented how financial incentives designed to "reward participation" end up exclusively benefiting those who already have capital. Acemoglu and Robinson (*Why Nations Fail*, 2012) showed that extractive institutions — those that concentrate benefits in an elite — destroy long-term growth, even when they start with good intentions.

**Who can invest USD 500 in VIN bonds?** The Venezuelan in Miami with a USD-denominated job, not the one in Bogota earning COP 1,300,000/month (USD ~300). A 2x dividend for investors creates **two classes of citizens from day 1** — exactly the opposite of a model with 40 million equal shareholders.
:::

### The problem with the original model

The initial design rewarded investment with a differentiated dividend:

- Investor (USD 500+): receives **2x** the base dividend
- Non-investor: receives **1x** the base dividend

This sounds reasonable in a startup. In a country, it's toxic. It turns the non-investor citizen into a second-class shareholder — for a reason they didn't choose (poverty).

### The solution: equal dividend + separate financial return

| Model | Citizen dividend | Investment return | Who benefits? |
|-------|-----------------|-------------------|---------------|
| **Previous model** (2x) | 2x for investors, 1x for the rest | Included in the dividend | Creates two classes of citizens |
| **Reformed model** | **Equal for everyone** — 40M people, same amount | Interest, bond appreciation, market returns | Citizenship equality + financial incentive intact |

### Reference: Alaska Permanent Fund

Every Alaska resident receives **the same PFD check** — [USD 1,702 in 2024](https://pfd.alaska.gov/) — regardless of whether they invest on their own or not. The fund has USD 80B in assets. Nobody receives a double dividend for being richer. Result: the PFD has **universal political support** — 730,000 defenders, not just those who invest.

### Political advantage: 40 million defenders

:::tip The equal dividend is a democratic lock
If 40 million people receive exactly the same dividend, **40 million people will defend the sovereign fund**. If only 2 million investors receive double, the other 38 million see it as "the fund for people with money." A sovereign fund without popular legitimacy won't survive the first populist who promises to distribute it.
:::

**The incentive to invest doesn't disappear.** Whoever buys citizen bonds or VIN shares receives market returns: interest, appreciation, financial dividends. That return is proportional to the investment — like any financial instrument. What is **not** proportional is citizenship.

## Anti-Concentration: Lessons from Eastern Europe

:::caution The ghost of Russian privatization
In the 1990s, both the Czech Republic and Russia used vouchers to privatize state-owned enterprises. The Czech Republic achieved an imperfect but functional result — reasonable diversification of ownership. Russia got **oligarchs**: 7 people controlled 50% of the economy by 1996. The difference wasn't the mechanism (both used vouchers). It was **the quality of institutions and anti-concentration safeguards**.
:::

### The concrete risk

Citizen bonds (starting at USD 10) and VIN shares (starting at USD 500) could end up concentrated if there are no barriers. An actor with USD 50M could buy bonds from thousands of desperate citizens at a discount — exactly what happened in Russia when vouchers were sold for the price of a bottle of vodka.

### 5 anti-concentration mechanisms

| # | Mechanism | What it prevents | Precedent |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-----------|
| 1 | **5-year lock-up** — citizen bonds non-transferable for 5 years from issuance | Fire sales to speculators in the early years (when desperation is greatest) | Standard IPO lock-ups; Chile AFP (non-withdrawable pension funds) |
| 2 | **Accumulation cap: 0.1% of total** — no person or entity can own more than 0.1% of total bonds issued | Russian-style oligarchic concentration | Pension fund antitrust regulation (Chile, Singapore) |
| 3 | **Programmatic smart contracts** — restrictions coded into digital issuance: non-transfer during lock-up, automatic accumulation limit, full traceability | Rule evasion through intermediaries or front men | Estonia e-Residency (digital identity); Singapore sovereign blockchain |
| 4 | **Mandatory KYC on every transfer** — if a person/entity buys bonds from 100+ different people, automatic investigation is triggered | Silent accumulation through fragmented purchases | FATF/GAFI AML standards; securities market regulation (SEC, CNMV) |
| 5 | **Progressive accumulation tax** — increasing rate on holdings that exceed thresholds (0.01% of total: 0%; 0.05%: 5%; 0.1%: 15%) | Economic incentive not to accumulate beyond what's reasonable | Wealth taxes (Norway, Spain); land ownership tax (post-war Japan) |

### Key difference: Czech Republic vs. Russia

| Factor | Czech Rep. | Russia | Venezuela S.A. |
|--------|-----------|--------|----------------|
| Regulatory institutions | Functional (Austro-Hungarian heritage) | Non-existent or captured | Simultaneous construction (Gate 1 of the plan) |
| Voucher transferability | Regulated, with intermediary funds | Free and immediate | 5-year lock-up + KYC + smart contracts |
| Privatization speed | Gradual (1992-1995) | Shock (loans-for-shares, 1995-96) | 15 years with verification gates |
| Outcome | Property-owning middle class (imperfect) | 7 oligarchs control 50% of the economy | Goal: 40M micro-owners |
