# Rule of Law, Legal Framework, and Monetary Reform

> Without legal certainty, no citizen bond is worth anything. Without a stable currency, no project survives.

## Legal Certainty

[USD 19,000M in claims](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/04/venezuelas-billions-in-distressed-debt-who-is-in-line-to-collect.html) against Citgo alone. More than [30 pending ICSID cases](https://www.cnbc.com/2026/01/04/venezuelas-billions-in-distressed-debt-who-is-in-line-to-collect.html) for expropriations from the Chavez/Maduro era.

| Reform | Action | Reference |
|--------|--------|-----------|
| Investment protection | Constitutional guarantee + ICSID re-accession | Chile: bilateral treaties |
| Digital property registry | Blockchain-based property titles | [Georgia: reformed in 2 years](https://centreforpublicimpact.org/public-impact-fundamentals/seizing-the-moment-rebuilding-georgias-police/) |
| Commercial courts | Judges with international experience, specialized jurisdiction | [Dubai DIFC Courts](https://www.difccourts.ae/) |
| Citizen bond protection | Constitutional rank + NY/London jurisdiction | Standard sovereign bonds |
| ICSID claims resolution | Compensation program or global settlement | See [Debt](/02-motor-financiero/deuda) |

### Legal Roadmap

| Phase | Action | Timeline |
|-------|--------|----------|
| Day 1 | Declaration of respect for private property and contracts | Immediate |
| Month 1–3 | ICSID re-accession + recognition of arbitral awards | Quarter 1 |
| Month 3–6 | Judicial reform: specialized commercial courts | Semester 1 |
| Year 1 | Digital property registry (Georgia/Estonia model) | Year 1 |
| Year 1–2 | Investment protection law (constitutional rank) | Legislature |
| Year 2–5 | Resolution of pending ICSID cases | Negotiation |

## Monetary Reform: Formal Dollarization

:::danger Monetary emergency
[IMF estimates 682% inflation for 2026](https://www.imf.org). The bolivar has lost 479% of its value in one year ([Euronews, Jan. 2026](https://www.euronews.com/business/2026/01/01/venezuelan-bolivardollar-rate-jumps-nearly-480-as-sanctions-bite-deepens)). Venezuela is already **dollarized de facto**: [~65–80% of transactions](https://globalization.guide/dollarization-of-venezuela) are conducted in USD or stablecoins (USDT). Formalization means recognizing what already exists.
:::

### Proposal: Formal Dollarization

**Model:** [Ecuador 2000](https://latamfdi.com/dollarization-in-ecuador-marks/) / [Panama 1904](https://latamfdi.com/dollarization-in-latin-america/)

### Dollarization Model Comparison

| Indicator | Ecuador (25 years) | Panama (122 years) | Argentina (Milei) | Venezuela (proposal) |
|-----------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|----------------------|
| Year of adoption | 2000 | 1904 | Did not adopt (abandoned promise) | Day 1 |
| Inflation pre-dollarization | 96.1% (2000) | N/A | 211% (2023) | 682% (2026 est.) |
| Inflation post (current) | [1.51% (Nov. 2024)](https://latamfdi.com/dollarization-in-ecuador-marks/) — lowest in LATAM | ~2% stable | 31.8% (Nov. 2025) | Target: <5% |
| Citizen approval | [~90%](https://latamfdi.com/dollarization-in-ecuador-marks/) after 25 years | Total (part of national identity) | N/A | — |
| Average GDP post | ~1.5%/year last decade | ~5%/year (pre-COVID) | +4.4% (2025) | Target: >4% |
| Foreign trade | Exports grew (shrimp: 1/4 of world consumption) | International financial center | Recovering | Base for forward contracts |

### Advantages of Dollarizing Venezuela

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Eliminates inflation** | Ecuador went from 96% to 1.5% in 25 years. Venezuela would go from 682% to <5% |
| **Simplifies citizen bonds** | Without exchange rate risk, bonds from USD 10 are viable |
| **Attracts investment** | Companies do not need currency hedging. Reduces cost of capital |
| **Restores confidence** | After decades of devaluations, the dollar is the only credible currency |
| **Already a de facto reality** | ~65–80% of transactions are already in USD. Just needs formalization |
| **Forward contracts in USD** | Oil contracts are already denominated in dollars |

### Risks of Dollarization

| Risk | Mitigation |
|------|------------|
| Loss of monetary policy | Oil provides a constant dollar flow. Sovereign Fund acts as stabilizer |
| No lender of last resort | [Panama solved this](https://latamfdi.com/dollarization-in-latin-america/) with a robust and well-regulated banking system |
| Dependence on the Federal Reserve | Shared with Ecuador/Panama — has not prevented their growth |
| Deflation if oil revenues fall | Floor price in forward contracts + stabilization fund |

:::info Milei's Lesson
[Argentina did NOT dollarize](https://www.realinstitutoelcano.org/en/analyses/argentina-under-currency-competition-the-future-of-exchange-rate-policy-and-dollarisation/) despite it being a campaign promise. It opted for currency competition + exchange rate bands. Result: inflation dropped from 211% to 31.8% but [did not eliminate exchange rate instability](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2026/argentinas-fragile-monetary-framework-risks-renewed-volatility). Venezuela has an advantage Argentina does not: a constant flow of oil dollars that makes dollarization sustainable.
:::
