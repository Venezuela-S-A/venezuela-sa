# Banking and Financial System: Rebuilding Credit

> Without functional banks there is no mortgage credit, no citizen bonds, no formal economy.

## Diagnosis

The Venezuelan banking system operates under extreme conditions:

| Problem | Detail | Source |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Reserve requirement | [73%](https://legalclarity.org/how-venezuelan-banks-operate-under-sanctions-and-hyperinflation/) (reduced from 85% in 2022) — banks cannot lend | Legal Clarity |
| International sanctions | [Banks cut off from the SWIFT system](https://legalclarity.org/how-venezuelan-banks-operate-under-sanctions-and-hyperinflation/) — no international correspondent banking | OFAC/Legal Clarity |
| Petro (state cryptocurrency) | [Shut down in January 2024](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Bank_of_Venezuela) — failed experiment | Government |
| De facto dollarization | [65–80% of transactions in USD](https://globalization.guide/dollarization-of-venezuela) or stablecoins (USDT) | Globalization Guide |
| Private sector credit | Practically nonexistent due to 73% reserve requirement | — |
| State-owned banks | Oversized, inefficient | — |

:::danger Without credit there is no economy
With a 73% reserve requirement, banks retain 73% of every deposit. There is no mortgage credit, no business credit, no SME financing. The economy runs on cash (USD) and cryptocurrencies. This is incompatible with citizen bonds, formal investment, and economic formalization.
:::

## Reform Plan

### Phase 1: Stabilization (Years 1–3)

| Action | Detail | Model |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Formal dollarization | Eliminates currency risk, enables USD intermediation | See [Rule of Law](/04-gobernanza/estado-derecho-moneda) |
| Gradual reserve requirement reduction | 73% → 40% → 20% (intl. standard) | Gradual, tied to inflation |
| SWIFT reconnection | OFAC negotiation post-sanctions | Requires political transition |
| Updated banking regulation | Basel III, independent supervision | [Panama: banking center](https://latamfdi.com/dollarization-in-latin-america/) |
| State bank audit | Identify viable banks vs. liquidate | — |

### Phase 2: Reconstruction (Years 3–7)

| Action | Detail | Model |
|--------|--------|-------|
| International banking licenses | Attract foreign banks (Santander, BBVA, Citi) | Colombia / Panama |
| Mortgage credit | State guarantee for social housing | Chile: housing subsidy |
| Digital banking | Platform for citizen bonds starting at USD 10 | [Nubank](https://www.nubank.com) / M-Pesa |
| Agricultural and industrial microcredit | State guarantee fund | Colombia: Bancoldex |
| Regulated fintech | Regulatory sandbox for financial startups | Singapore MAS |

### Phase 3: Maturity (Years 7–15)

| Action | Detail | Model |
|--------|--------|-------|
| Regional financial center | International banking zone in Caracas | Panama: 90+ international banks |
| Functional stock exchange | Listing of tech companies from VIN portfolio | — |
| Insurance and reinsurance | Market to cover investment risks | Lloyd's / Bermuda |

## Digital Banking: Account in 10 Minutes

**Target:** Any Venezuelan opens a USD bank account from their phone in **under 10 minutes**. No paperwork. No branch. No lines.

**Prerequisites:**
- Formal dollarization (see [Rule of Law and Currency](/04-gobernanza/estado-derecho-moneda))
- Verifiable digital identity — digital ID with biometrics (see [Digital State](/06-realidad/estado-digital))
- Clear fintech regulation — operational sandbox

**Reference:** [Nubank](https://www.nu.com.br/) (Brazil) opened **100M+ accounts** from a cellphone in a country with inefficient banking. [Uala](https://www.uala.com.ar/) (Argentina) grew during **200%** inflation — proving that crisis doesn't prevent fintech adoption, it accelerates it. [Stripe](https://stripe.com/) (Collison): *"With dollarization + digital ID + clear regulation, Stripe operates in 6 months."*

| Service | Current state | Target | Timeline |
|---------|--------------|--------|----------|
| **Account opening** | Days/weeks with in-person paperwork | **10 minutes** from app | Year 1–2 |
| **Payments** | Cash-heavy, informal, 70%+ in physical USD | **Instant digital payments** in USD | Year 1 |
| **Remittances** | 5–8% commission per transfer | **1–2% via app** (Wise/Remitly model) | Year 1 |
| **Credit** | Nonexistent for 80%+ (73% reserve requirement) | **Algorithmic scoring** — credit based on digital history | Year 2–3 |
| **Savings** | No incentive exists (inflation destroys) | **USD accounts with yield** from sovereign fund | Year 2–3 |
| **Insurance** | ~0% penetration | **Parametric insurance** via app (health, agriculture, micro) | Year 2–3 |

:::tip The Venezuelan paradox: the crisis is the opportunity
Venezuela has no banking legacy to protect. There are no 1990s core banking systems to migrate. There is no strong banking lobby to block fintech. It can go **straight to digital banking** without going through 20th-century banking. Uala proved it in Argentina: the crisis destroyed trust in traditional banks and accelerated digital adoption.
:::

## 4 Prerequisites for the Investment Platform

> Patrick Collison (Stripe): *"Without these 4, there's no platform. With them, Stripe operates in 6 months."*

The citizen investment platform (sovereign bonds starting at USD 10, VIN portfolio shares) requires infrastructure that **does not exist today**:

| # | Prerequisite | Current state | Required action | Timeline |
|---|-------------|---------------|-----------------|----------|
| 1 | **Functional banking system** | Collapsed — 73% reserve requirement, no SWIFT correspondent banking | Recapitalize + license **3–5 digital banks** + reconnect SWIFT | Year 1 |
| 2 | **Formal dollarization** | Informal ~70%+ of transactions in USD | Formalize by decree + bankarize USD deposits | Day 1–30 |
| 3 | **Clear payment regulation** | Nonexistent for fintech | Draft + approve **fintech sandbox** ([MAS Singapore](https://www.mas.gov.sg/) model) | Year 1 |
| 4 | **Verifiable digital identity** | Paper ID, analog civil registry | **Biometric digital ID** — [Estonia eID](https://e-estonia.com/solutions/e-identity/id-card/) model | Year 1–2 |

:::info Sequence matters
The sequence is: **(1) dollarization → (2) digital identity → (3) regulation → (4) digital banks → investment platform**. All 4 can advance in parallel, but the investment platform does not launch without all 4 operational. The most aggressive timeline is **18–24 months** from Day 1.
:::

## ICSID + Leapfrogging: Jump to the 21st Century

### ICSID: Back to the Table

Venezuela [withdrew from ICSID](https://icsid.worldbank.org/) (International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes) in **2012** under Chavez. Result: international investors have no protection against expropriation → they don't invest.

| What ICSID provides | Why it matters |
|---------------------|----------------|
| International arbitration for investors | Without this, any contract is worthless — the investor has no recourse |
| Protection against expropriation | Venezuela expropriated USD 20B+ in assets 2007–2014; ICSID is the antidote |
| Credibility signal | Being in ICSID = "this country respects contracts." Not being in = "run" |
| Access to multilateral financing | IDB, CAF, IFC condition projects on legal investor protection |

**Action:** Rejoin ICSID within the first **90 days**. It's a formality + maximum-impact political signal.

### Leapfrogging: Skip 20th-Century Banking

Venezuela doesn't need to rebuild the traditional banking system. It can **skip it**.

> Cathie Wood (ARK Invest): *"If Venezuela jumps straight to digital government + AI + crypto without going through legacy banking, that's a 20–50x investment case."*

| Country | What they skipped | Result | Lesson for Venezuela |
|---------|------------------|--------|---------------------|
| **Kenya** | No bank branches | [M-Pesa](https://www.vodafone.com/about-vodafone/what-we-do/consumer-products-and-services/m-pesa): **70%+ financial inclusion** via mobile | You don't need physical banks; you need an app + agent network |
| **India** | Slow, expensive bank transfers | [UPI](https://www.npci.org.in/what-we-do/upi/product-overview): **10B transactions/month**, USD 0 cost | Public + private payment infrastructure = massive adoption |
| **Brazil** | Expensive inter-bank transfers | [Pix](https://www.bcb.gov.br/en/financialstability/pix_en): **USD 0 cost, instant**, 150M+ users | The central bank can create the rails; the private sector builds on top |
| **Venezuela** | Collapsed banking system | **?** → 40M on fintech Day 1 | The crisis destroyed the legacy. There's nothing to protect. Only to build |

:::caution This is not optional — it's the strategy
If Venezuela tries to rebuild traditional banking (branches, 1990s core banking, paper processes) it loses 10 years and USD 5B. If it jumps straight to fintech + digital payments + digital identity, it arrives in 2–3 years where Brazil arrived in 10. **The collapse is the advantage.**
:::
