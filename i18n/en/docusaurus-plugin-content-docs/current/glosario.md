# Glossary of Terms

> Quick reference for all terms, models, and acronyms used in the plan.

---

## Plan Structure

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Venezuela S.A.** | Corporate holding of 40 million citizen-shareholders. Invests in JVs, collects royalties, manages the Venezuela S.A. Investment Fund, and distributes dividends. It is NOT the State — it is the citizens' company |
| **State** | Regulatory and supervisory entity. Only 5 functions: government, healthcare (supervises), justice, education (supervises), security. It does not operate businesses or services |
| **Venezuela S.A. Investment Fund** | Investment fund managed by Venezuela S.A. that accumulates oil, mining, and diversification revenues. Target: USD 250-470B in 15 years. Returns are distributed as citizen dividend |
| **Citizen Dividend** | Annual direct payment to every Venezuelan from the returns of the Venezuela S.A. Investment Fund (10-40% of returns depending on phase). Model: [Alaska Permanent Fund](https://pfd.alaska.gov/) |
| **Pre-Seed** | First funding round of the plan. USD 39.5M from the diaspora (79,000 investors x USD 500 average). Starts without government |
| **CVP** | Citizen Value Package. Combines 7 components: healthcare + education + employment + infrastructure + security + property title + dividend. The real value for the citizen |
| **FCV** | Citizen Fund Venezuela. Mandatory unified personal account ([Singapore CPF](https://www.cpf.gov.sg/)-style) with 5 sub-accounts: Retirement (8%), Health (7%), Housing (4%), Education (2%), Severance (2%). Total: 23% of salary (11% worker + 12% employer). One institution, one account, one app. Evolves from 14% (emergency phase) to 27% (maturity) |
| **Venezuela S.A. Investment Fund** | Collective investment fund ([Norway NBIM](https://www.nbim.no/)-style) managed by Venezuela S.A. Receives oil, mining, gas, and JV royalty revenues. Invests 100% outside Venezuela. Distributes annual citizen dividend. **Do not confuse with the FCV** — the Investment Fund is collective, the FCV is personal |

## Healthcare

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **FONASA** | National Health Fund (Chile model). Mandatory universal insurance funded by 7% salary contribution. Everyone contributes, everyone is covered. [fonasa.cl](https://www.fonasa.cl/) |
| **Tramo A** | FONASA category for people with no income or in extreme poverty. Full coverage, 0% copay |
| **Tramo B** | FONASA category for low-income people. Full coverage, 0% copay |
| **Tramo C** | FONASA category for middle income. 10% copay on services |
| **Tramo D** | FONASA category for high income. 20% copay on services |
| **Copay** | Percentage of a medical service's cost that the patient pays out of pocket. Tramos A/B: 0%. Tramo C: 10%. Tramo D: 20% |
| **ISAPRE** | Private Health Insurance Institutions (Chile model). Private health insurance. Voluntary option for those who want more coverage or shorter waits. Does not replace FONASA — it complements it |
| **Medisave** | Individual savings component of the FCV Health Sub-account ([Singapore](https://www.moh.gov.sg/) model). Gradually introduced from year 5: the 7% health contribution is split between the common contributory fund and an individual account. It is not a separate system — it is part of the FCV |
| **FONASA + Medisave Hybrid Model** | Gradual transition: Years 1-5: 100% contributory FONASA. Years 5-10: 5% FONASA + 2% Medisave. Years 10-15: 4% FONASA + 3% Medisave. Years 15+: 3% FONASA + 4% Medisave |
| **BOT Concession** | Build-Operate-Transfer. Model where the private sector builds and operates a hospital (or infrastructure) for 20-30 years and then transfers it to the State/Venezuela S.A. |

## Education

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Universal Voucher (K-12)** | Allocation that every child and young person receives to pay for tuition + transport + extracurricular activities at the school of their choice (public, private, online). It is a right — not merit-based |
| **University Voucher** | Allocation for higher education. Unlike K-12, it is **earned and maintained by merit**: grades, admission exam, semester performance. Failure to meet requirements means losing the voucher (not the seat) |
| **SEP** | Preferential School Subsidy (Chile model). Differentiated voucher: low-income families receive a **50% larger** voucher. This makes schools compete to attract low-income students because they bring more funding |
| **Extracurricular Voucher** | Portion of the voucher designated for activities outside the academic curriculum: languages, music, theater, visual arts, martial arts, sports, robotics, programming. Parents choose accredited providers |
| **School as Autonomous Enterprise** | Model where public schools transition to private enterprises managed by the educational community. They compete for vouchers. They receive modernization funds and technical assistance for the transition |
| **Online School** | Digital school with official accreditation. Operates with a network of in-person and remote tutors for personalized support. Valid option equivalent to in-person schooling |
| **Accreditation** | Quality certification that enables a school or university to receive vouchers. Based on outcomes (employability, grades, satisfaction). If lost, the market loses trust and the institution must take action to recover it |
| **Quality Agency** | Supervisory entity that audits schools and universities on academic results, infrastructure, and management. Model: [Chile Education Quality Agency](https://www.agenciaeducacion.cl/) |
| **PIRLS** | Progress in International Reading Literacy Study. International reading assessment at age 10. Singapore #1 worldwide. Reference for the reading pillar of the Venezuelan curriculum |
| **PISA** | Programme for International Student Assessment (OECD). Assessment of mathematics, science, and reading at age 15. Venezuela has not participated since 2009. Target: rejoin and reach the LATAM average |
| **ISA** | Income Share Agreement. Bootcamp payment model: the student pays no tuition; they pay a % of their salary (15-20%) for 2 years after getting a job |

## Finance and Fiscal

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Flat Tax** | Single-rate tax. Venezuela proposes 15% for individuals and companies. Model: [Estonia](https://www.emta.ee/en), [Georgia](https://www.rs.ge/) |
| **VAT** | Value Added Tax. Proposal: 12% (vs. Chile 19%, Colombia 19%). Basic basket exempt |
| **SEEZ** | Special Economic Zone for Technology. Areas with 0% corporate tax for 10 years + 30-year regulatory stability. Model: [Shenzhen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shenzhen), Dubai, [Argentina RIGI](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2025/10/30/bcpargentina-RIGI-foreign-invetments-report/1561761834454/) |
| **Forward Contract** | Agreement for advance sale of oil at a fixed price. Allows Venezuela S.A. to secure future revenues and obtain immediate financing against future production |
| **Floor Price** | Guaranteed minimum price in a forward contract. The plan uses USD 55/barrel as floor |
| **Base Price** | USD 60/barrel. Every calculation in the plan uses this price. Anything above goes to the Venezuela S.A. Investment Fund as upside |

## Pensions

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **AFP** | Pension Fund Administrator (Chile model, historical reference). In Venezuela, pensions are the **FCV Retirement Sub-account** — not a separate AFP system. Contribution: 8% initial, rising to 10% at maturity, within the unified FCV of 21-25% |
| **CPF** | Central Provident Fund (Singapore model). Mandatory multi-purpose account: retirement + housing + healthcare. Contribution 37%. Ranked #5 globally. Long-term reference |
| **Pillar 1** | Universal basic pension funded by taxes. For EVERY retiree from Day 1. Target: USD 100-200/month. Covers those who never contributed |
| **Pillar 2** | Mandatory individual savings in AFP/CPF. The worker accumulates their own pension. Worker's property |
| **Pillar 3** | Additional voluntary savings. Tax incentives to save beyond the mandatory amount |
| **Replacement Rate** | Percentage of the last salary that the pension replaces. Chile AFP: ~40%. Singapore CPF: ~70%. Venezuela target: >50% |

## Governance

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Tripartite Oversight** | Governance model where service quality (healthcare, education) is supervised by three actors: State (standards and auditing) + Community (local committees) + Users (free choice and surveys) |
| **JV** | Joint Venture. A joint enterprise where Venezuela S.A. contributes land/resources/permits as equity and the private partner contributes capital/technology/management. Venezuela S.A. is a **shareholder**, not an owner |
| **Debswana Model** | Reference for JVs: Botswana (50%) + De Beers (50%) created Debswana to exploit diamonds. Botswana went from being one of the poorest countries to having the highest GDP per capita in Africa |
| **Santiago Principles** | 24 governance principles for sovereign funds defined by the [International Forum of Sovereign Wealth Funds](https://www.ifswf.org/). Include transparency, political independence, and accountability |
| **ICSID** | International Centre for Settlement of Investment Disputes ([World Bank](https://icsid.worldbank.org/)). Arbitration tribunal for disputes between investors and states. Venezuela withdrew in 2012 — the plan contemplates re-entry |
| **OFAC** | Office of Foreign Assets Control (U.S.). Administers sanctions. Controls which companies can operate with Venezuela and under what conditions |

## Infrastructure and Services

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **Concession** | Contract where the State/Venezuela S.A. grants the private sector the right to build and operate infrastructure (highways, ports, hospitals) for 20-30 years. Model: [Chile MOP](https://www.mop.cl/) |
| **PPP** | Public-Private Partnership. Public-private association for infrastructure. Venezuela S.A. as shareholder in JV, private sector as operator |
| **BOT** | Build-Operate-Transfer. The private sector builds, operates for the concession period, and transfers at the end |
| **Cost-Reflective Tariff** | Price of a service (water, electricity, gas) that reflects the real cost of production and distribution. No subsidy. Venezuelan hydro is naturally cheap (~USD 0.05-0.06/kWh) |
| **Targeted Voucher** | Monetary aid directed ONLY to people in verified extreme poverty (5-10% of the population) for basic services (water, electricity). Not a universal subsidy |

## Oil and Energy

| Term | Definition |
|------|-----------|
| **bpd** | Barrels per day. Current production: ~0.9-1.1M bpd. Target: 3M bpd in 15 years ([Rystad Energy](https://www.rigzone.com/news/could_venezuela_production_get_back_to_3mm_barrels_per_day-08-jan-2026-182716-article/)) |
| **Orinoco Belt** | Largest oil accumulation in the world. Extra-heavy crude requiring dilution and special processing. Extraction cost: USD 35-40/barrel |
| **Rystad Energy** | Norwegian energy sector consultancy of reference. Its investment and timeline estimates for Venezuela are the basis of the plan |
| **Royalty** | Percentage of production value that the resource owner (Venezuela S.A.) charges the operator (oil/mining company) for the right to extract |
| **Caroni Cascade** | Hydroelectric system in Bolivar state: Guri, Macagua, Caruachi, Tocoma. 18,000 MW capacity. 74% of Venezuela's electricity is hydroelectric |

## International Reference Models

| Model | Country | What We Take |
|-------|---------|-------------|
| **FONASA** | Chile | Universal healthcare with mandatory 7% contribution. Income-based tramos |
| **Medisave / 3M** | Singapore | Individual medical savings. Efficiency: 5% GDP with first-world outcomes |
| **CPF** | Singapore | Pensions with high contribution (37%), multi-purpose individual accounts |
| **AFP** | Chile | Individual pension accounts with freedom of fund choice |
| **SEP** | Chile | Differentiated educational voucher: +50% for low income |
| **MOP Concessions** | Chile | 82+ infrastructure concessions in 30 years. Best PPP program in LATAM |
| **e-Estonia** | Estonia | 100% digital government. 99% of procedures online. Saves 2% GDP |
| **ProgeTiger** | Estonia | Programming from 1st grade in all schools |
| **Police Reform** | Georgia | Eliminated police corruption in 2 years. Dismissed and rehired the entire force |
| **NREGA** | India | Guaranteed employment 100 days/year in public works. 50M households/year |
| **De Soto** | Peru | Massive titling of informal properties. Unlocks capital |
| **Plan Ceibal** | Uruguay | 1 laptop per child. 100% of schools with internet in 5 years |
| **Debswana** | Botswana | 50/50 JV with De Beers for diamonds. Transformed the poorest country in Africa |
| **Norway Fund** | Norway | USD 2.2T oil investment fund. 30 years. Governance model |
