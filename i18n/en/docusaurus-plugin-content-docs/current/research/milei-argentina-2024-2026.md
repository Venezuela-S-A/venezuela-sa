## 2. State Reform: The Chainsaw

### Downsizing the state apparatus
- **9 of 18 ministries eliminated** — 50% reduction in cabinet size
- **~48,000 public employees laid off** (Dec. 2023 – Apr. 2025), 7% reduction of state payroll
- **2,700+ public works frozen**
- **3.5 regulations eliminated per day** on average
- Creation of the **Ministry of Deregulation** to dismantle bureaucracy

Sources: [Buenos Aires Times](https://www.batimes.com.ar/news/economy/mileis-chainsaw-cut-nearly-35000-public-sector-jobs-in-2024.phtml); [Flossbach von Storch](https://www.flossbachvonstorch-researchinstitute.com/en/studies/detail/argentina-under-the-reforms-of-javier-milei-taking-stock/); [Heritage Foundation](https://www.heritage.org/budget-and-spending/commentary/mileis-argentine-triumph-shows-why-musks-doge-revolution-isnt-enough)

### Privatizations
- **Aerolineas Argentinas** and **Enarsa** (energy) privatized
- **Ley Bases (2024):** legal framework to privatize up to 41 state-owned enterprises
- Sectors affected: energy, mining, transportation, telecommunications

Source: [AS/COA](https://www.as-coa.org/articles/status-check-president-javier-mileis-policy-proposals)

### Deregulation
- Elimination of price controls (milk, sugar, cooking gas)
- Liberalization of airline, real estate, and transportation markets
- Reduction of tariffs and import taxes
- Simplified business registration

## 4. Monetary Policy: Dollarization Abandoned, Currency Competition

### What he promised vs. what he did

| Campaign promise | Reality |
|-----------------|---------|
| Full dollarization | **Abandoned.** Opted for currency competition |
| Close the Central Bank (BCRA) | **No.** BCRA still operating with greater independence |
| Eliminate the peso | **No.** De facto bi-monetary system |

Source: [Real Instituto Elcano](https://www.realinstitutoelcano.org/en/analyses/argentina-under-currency-competition-the-future-of-exchange-rate-policy-and-dollarisation/)

### Monetary timeline
1. **Dec. 2023:** 50% peso devaluation (shock therapy)
2. **Apr. 2025:** Elimination of currency controls for individuals (cepo lifted)
3. **Apr. 2025:** IMF agreement for **USD 20B** (48 months), USD 12B immediate
4. **Oct. 2025:** Currency swap **USD 20B** with U.S. (Bessent)
5. **Jan. 2026:** New inflation-adjusted exchange rate band scheme

Sources: [PIIE](https://www.piie.com/blogs/realtime-economics/2026/argentinas-fragile-monetary-framework-risks-renewed-volatility); [Funds Society](https://www.fundssociety.com/en/news/markets/argentina-modifies-its-exchange-rate-scheme-how-far-will-it-go/); [American Conservative](https://www.theamericanconservative.com/milei-lifts-argentinas-currency-controls/)

### Lesson for Venezuela
Formal dollarization (proposed in Venezuela S.A.) was not implemented even in Argentina. Milei opted for a gradual path: devaluation --> stabilization --> currency competition --> exchange rate bands. **Evidence that full dollarization is politically difficult even with an ideologically committed president.**

- Investments >USD 200M
- Corporate tax: 25% (vs. 35% standard)
- Regulatory stability for 30 years

## 9. Lessons for Venezuela S.A.

### What IS applicable

| Lesson from Argentina | Application in Venezuela |
|----------------------|--------------------------|
| Fiscal surplus as a credibility anchor | The sovereign fund needs fiscal discipline from Day 1 |
| Massive deregulation attracts investment | ZEETs can use the RIGI model (30-year stability) |
| State reduction works (18 --> 9 ministries) | Venezuela needs radical state reform (Georgia + Milei model) |
| Vaca Muerta as extractive model | Venezuela has the Orinoco Belt + Guri as an energy combo |
| IMF agreement as market signal | Venezuela will need an IMF program post-restructuring |
| Market re-entry after 8 years of exclusion | Venezuela has been in default for 9 years — the sequence is similar |

### What is NOT applicable or requires caution

| Lesson | Why it doesn't apply directly |
|--------|-------------------------------|
| Dollarization abandoned | Venezuela is already de facto dollarized (~60–70% of transactions in USD). Formalization may be easier |
| Social austerity shock | Venezuela already has 80% poverty. There is no margin for worsening — the sequence must be different |
| Negative FDI despite reforms | Reforms don't guarantee immediate investment. Forward contracts are the correct mechanism for Venezuela |
| Industry devastated | Venezuela has no manufacturing industry to destroy — it can build from scratch |
| 300,000 jobs lost | Venezuela needs to CREATE jobs, not eliminate them (except PDVSA reform) |

### Proposed hybrid model

Venezuela can adopt from Milei:
1. **Fiscal discipline** (constitutional rule for surplus or spending limited to 3–4% of the fund)
2. **Deregulation** (Venezuelan RIGI for ZEETs: 0% tax for 10 years, 30-year stability)
3. **Radical transparency** (publication of all contracts, DOGE-style auditing)
4. **Monetary pragmatism** (formalize dollarization, not half-measures like Argentina)

But must avoid:
1. **Social shock without a safety net** (Phase 0 humanitarian response is an absolute precondition)
2. **Industrial destruction** (Venezuela needs to build, not cut)
3. **Dependency on a single leader** (constitutional rules > political will)

## 10. Executive Summary

**In 2 years, Milei achieved:**
- Inflation: 211% --> 31.8%
- Deficit: -2.7% GDP --> +1.8% GDP (first surplus in 14 years)
- GDP: +4.4% in 2025
- Poverty: 42% --> 31.6%
- Country risk: >2,000 --> 630 bps
- 48,000 public jobs eliminated
- 9 ministries eliminated
- First international debt issuance in 8 years
- Landslide electoral victory (>40%)

**At what cost:**
- Poverty spiked to 53% before declining
- Industry fell 7.9% (2nd worst globally)
- Pensions lost half their purchasing power
- 300,000 formal jobs lost
- Negative net FDI in 2025
- Dollarization — the flagship promise — was abandoned

**Verdict for Venezuela S.A.:** Argentina demonstrates that radical reforms can work with fiscal discipline and deregulation, but the social cost is brutal without safety nets. Venezuela must reverse the sequence: **humanitarian safety net first (Phase 0), then fiscal adjustment.**
