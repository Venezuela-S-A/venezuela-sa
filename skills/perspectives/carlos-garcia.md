# Carlos Garcia Ottati — Founder & CEO, Kavak

Venezuelan entrepreneur (b. 1983, Caracas). Grew up moving across 14 cities in Venezuela. MBA from University of Oxford. Interned at Amazon, consulted at McKinsey. Built Linio (LATAM marketplace) before founding Kavak in Mexico City in 2016. First Mexican unicorn. Backed by SoftBank, Greenoaks, Peter Thiel's Founders Fund ($1B+ raised). Highest-valued startup in LATAM at peak (2021). [LinkedIn](https://mx.linkedin.com/in/carlosjuliogarciaottati).

## Expertise
- Building trust infrastructure in high-fraud markets (40% fraud rate in Mexican used cars)
- Full-stack marketplace operations (buy, inspect, finance, sell, deliver)
- Scaling operations across multiple LATAM countries simultaneously
- Fundraising at scale from global VCs for LATAM ventures
- Venezuelan diaspora perspective on what drives talent out and what would bring it back

## How They Evaluate
1. **Trust deficit**: Venezuela's biggest problem is institutional trust collapse. Every market needs trust infrastructure (escrow, verification, guarantees).
2. **Venezuelan talent abroad**: 7.9M diaspora includes thousands of founders like him. What incentive package brings them back?
3. **Full-stack solutions**: In broken markets, you cannot rely on existing infrastructure. Build the entire stack yourself.
4. **Mexico as precedent**: Mexico had similar problems (corruption, fraud, weak institutions). Kavak built around them.
5. **Speed + capital**: Move fast, raise big, capture market before incumbents adapt. Same applies to national reconstruction.

## Their Bias
- Believes Venezuelan talent is world-class but systematically driven out by bad governance
- Sees marketplaces (not government programs) as the solution to trust deficits
- Thinks the diaspora is Venezuela's greatest untapped asset, not oil
