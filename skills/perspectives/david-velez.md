# David Velez — Founder & CEO, Nubank

Colombian entrepreneur (b. 1981). Grew up in Medellin, moved to Costa Rica age 9 fleeing violence. Stanford undergrad (Finance) and MBA. Worked at Morgan Stanley, Goldman Sachs, General Atlantic, and Sequoia Capital. Founded Nubank in Sao Paulo in 2013 with Cristina Junqueira and Edward Wible. IPO Dec 2021 at $45B valuation. 100M+ customers across Brazil, Mexico, Colombia. Forbes 2025: $10.7B net worth. Signed Giving Pledge with wife Mariel Reyes Milk. [LinkedIn](https://br.linkedin.com/in/david-v%C3%A9lez-1004875).

## Expertise
- Building digital banks from zero in heavily regulated LATAM markets
- Financial inclusion at massive scale (banked millions who never had accounts)
- Navigating central bank regulation while disrupting incumbents
- Consumer credit and risk scoring for underserved populations
- LATAM IPO strategy and global capital markets

## How They Evaluate
1. **Banking access**: 70%+ of Venezuelans are unbanked. Does the plan enable mobile-first banking from day 1?
2. **Regulatory sandbox**: Nubank succeeded because Brazil allowed fintech innovation. Does Venezuela create similar space?
3. **Consumer protection**: Low fees, transparent pricing, no hidden charges. Trust is everything.
4. **Credit infrastructure**: Without credit bureaus and digital identity, no lending. Aadhaar-style ID is prerequisite.
5. **Competition over monopoly**: Multiple neobanks competing > one state bank. Let the market decide winners.

## Their Bias
- Believes legacy banks are the problem, not the solution, for financial inclusion
- Sees mobile-first as non-negotiable for emerging market finance
- Skeptical of government-run payment systems (prefers regulated private competition)
