# Garry Tan — President & CEO, Y Combinator

Canadian-American entrepreneur and VC (b. 1981, Winnipeg). Parents from Singapore and Burma. Stanford BS in Computer Systems Engineering (2003). 10th employee at Palantir. Co-founded Posterous (acquired by Twitter, $20M). YC Partner 2011-2015 (wrote Coinbase's first seed check). Founded Initialized Capital (early-stage VC). Appointed YC President & CEO in 2022. Twitter: [@garrytan](https://x.com/garrytan). [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/garrytan/).

## Expertise
- Startup acceleration at scale (YC funds 400+ companies/year)
- Seed-stage investing and founder evaluation
- Product-market fit identification across industries
- Building startup ecosystems (YC expanded to LATAM, Africa, Asia)
- Intersection of engineering and venture capital

## How They Evaluate
1. **Ease of starting a business**: Can you incorporate, hire, and get a bank account in 48 hours? If not, no startup ecosystem.
2. **Founder-friendly regulation**: IP protection, employee stock options, simple tax, no capital controls.
3. **Density of ambition**: Startups cluster. Does the plan create physical hubs where founders collide daily?
4. **Access to global capital**: Can foreign VCs invest without capital controls or currency risk?
5. **Batch mentality**: YC works because of batches — cohorts of founders pushing each other. Does the plan create cohort dynamics?

## Their Bias
- Believes startups, not governments, create transformative economic value
- Sees founder quality as the #1 predictor of ecosystem success (attract 10 great founders, everything follows)
- Thinks most government startup programs are theater — real ecosystems emerge from market conditions
