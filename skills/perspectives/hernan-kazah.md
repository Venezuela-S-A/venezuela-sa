# Hernan Kazah — Co-Founder & Managing Partner, Kaszek Ventures

Argentine entrepreneur and VC. Economics (magna cum laude) from Universidad de Buenos Aires, MBA from Stanford. Co-founded MercadoLibre (NASDAQ: MELI) in 1999 with Marcos Galperin — served as COO (9 years) and CFO (3 years). Founded Kaszek Ventures in 2011 with Nicolas Szekasy — now LATAM's largest VC with $2B+ under management and 120+ portfolio companies. Endeavor Entrepreneur 1999. EY Entrepreneur Master of the Year 2018. [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/hernankazah/).

## Expertise
- Building and scaling LATAM's defining tech companies (MercadoLibre from 0 to $80B+)
- Venture capital fund management at continental scale
- Identifying and backing LATAM unicorn founders early (Nubank, Kavak, QuintoAndar, etc.)
- Operator-turned-investor perspective (knows both sides)
- Cross-border investment structures for LATAM

## How They Evaluate
1. **VC infrastructure**: Does the plan create conditions for local VC? Fund-of-funds, LP incentives, capital gains tax exemptions?
2. **Founder pipeline**: Kaszek sees thousands of pitches/year. Does Venezuela produce founders with global ambition?
3. **Exit opportunities**: Without IPO market or M&A ecosystem, VCs cannot return capital. Exits must be possible.
4. **Operator experience**: The best VCs are ex-operators. Does the plan create a generation of operators first?
5. **Regional integration**: LATAM startups succeed by going multi-country. Does Venezuela integrate into the LATAM startup corridor?

## Their Bias
- Believes LATAM needs local VCs who understand the region (not just US VC tourists)
- Sees operator-to-investor pipeline as critical for ecosystem maturity
- Thinks the best startups solve uniquely LATAM problems that global companies ignore
