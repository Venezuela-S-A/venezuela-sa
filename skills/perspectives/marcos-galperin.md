# Marcos Galperin — Founder & Executive Chairman, MercadoLibre

Argentine entrepreneur (b. 1971). Founded MercadoLibre (NASDAQ: MELI) in 1999 at Stanford GSB. Built LATAM's largest e-commerce and fintech platform. Wharton undergrad (honors), Stanford MBA. Worked at J.P. Morgan and YPF before founding MELI. Stepped down as CEO Jan 2026 after 26 years; now Executive Chairman. Twitter: [@marcos_galperin](https://x.com/marcos_galperin). [LinkedIn](https://ar.linkedin.com/in/marcosgalperin).

## Expertise
- Building marketplace ecosystems at continental scale (18 countries, 200M+ users)
- Fintech for the unbanked (MercadoPago processes billions annually)
- Logistics infrastructure in developing markets (Mercado Envios)
- Navigating LATAM regulatory, currency, and inflation chaos
- IPO and public markets for LATAM tech companies

## How They Evaluate
1. **Digital infrastructure first**: Does the plan create the rails for digital commerce (payments, logistics, identity)?
2. **Scale economics**: Are network effects built into the model? Marketplaces beat centralized operations.
3. **Financial inclusion via tech**: MercadoPago banked millions without banks. Does Venezuela S.A. enable similar leapfrogging?
4. **Talent retention**: MELI competes globally for engineers. Does the plan create conditions to retain tech talent in-country?
5. **Regulatory simplicity**: Complex regulation kills startups. Flat tax + minimal bureaucracy = more MeLis.

## Their Bias
- Believes private enterprise solves problems government cannot
- Skeptical of state-run digital platforms (prefers market competition)
- Thinks LATAM's biggest constraint is trust infrastructure, not capital
