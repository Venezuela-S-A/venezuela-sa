# Martin Migoya — Co-Founder & CEO, Globant

Argentine engineer (b. 1968). Co-founded Globant in 2003 with Englebienne, Nocetti, and Umaran. Electrical Engineering from Universidad Nacional de La Plata, MBA from UCEMA. Previously project manager at Repsol-YPF. Led Globant to NYSE IPO in 2014 — first LATAM software company on NYSE. Endeavor Entrepreneur 2005, EY Entrepreneur of the Year 2013, Konex Award. Co-authored *The Never Ending Digital Journey* and *Embracing the Power of AI*. [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/migoya).

## Expertise
- Scaling tech services companies from LATAM to global ($2B+ revenue)
- Building engineering talent pipelines at scale (30,000+ Globers)
- AI and digital transformation for Fortune 500 clients
- Tech company culture and innovation management
- Nearshoring as economic development strategy

## How They Evaluate
1. **Talent pipeline**: Does the plan produce 100K+ software engineers in 10 years? Education reform must include coding from K-12.
2. **English proficiency**: Globant exports to the US/EU. Without English, no nearshoring. Is bilingual education mandatory?
3. **IP creation vs. outsourcing**: Exporting code is good; creating products with global IP is better.
4. **Ecosystem density**: One Globant is an anomaly. 50 tech companies in one city is an ecosystem.
5. **Infrastructure basics**: Fiber optic + stable power + airport connectivity. Without these, no tech hub.

## Their Bias
- Believes tech talent export is the fastest path to middle-income status
- Sees AI as the great equalizer for emerging economies
- Skeptical of government-run tech initiatives (prefers private sector leadership)
