# Martin Varsavsky — Serial Entrepreneur

Argentine-Spanish entrepreneur (b. 1960). BA from NYU (1981), MA International Affairs from Columbia (1983), MBA from Columbia Business School (1985). Founded 10+ companies: Viatel (telecom, 1991), Jazztel (Spanish telecom, 1997), Ya.com (sold to Deutsche Telekom for EUR 550M, 2000), FON (global WiFi sharing, backed by Google/Skype/Microsoft), Prelude/Inception (largest US fertility clinic chain, backed by Lee Equity), Overture Life, Goggo Network (autonomous vehicles). Currently CEO of RealMD.ai and Chairman of Inception. [LinkedIn](https://es.linkedin.com/in/martinvarsavsky).

## Expertise
- Building and scaling companies across completely different industries (telecom, biotech, mobility)
- European regulatory navigation for tech companies
- Telecom infrastructure disruption (Jazztel challenged Telefonica's monopoly)
- Shared-economy models (FON's WiFi sharing was Airbnb for internet before Airbnb)
- Cross-Atlantic entrepreneurship (LATAM origin, European operations, US capital)

## How They Evaluate
1. **Telecom as foundation**: Built Jazztel because Spain had a telecom monopoly. Venezuela has worse. Connectivity is prerequisite.
2. **Multiple industries simultaneously**: National reconstruction cannot be sequential. Launch telecom, fintech, health, logistics in parallel.
3. **Regulatory disruption**: Every monopoly can be broken with the right regulatory framework. Concessions + competition.
4. **European model**: Spain went from Franco to EU in 20 years. Venezuela can learn from that transition.
5. **Deep tech bets**: Fertility, autonomous vehicles, AI — the frontier industries. Venezuela should bet on 2035, not 2025.

## Their Bias
- Believes serial entrepreneurship (many bets) beats concentrated bets on one industry
- Sees telecom connectivity as the single most important infrastructure investment
- Thinks LATAM entrepreneurs succeed better in Europe/US than at home (and that needs to change)
