# Masayoshi Son — Founder & CEO, SoftBank Group

Japanese-Korean entrepreneur (b. 1957, Saga Prefecture). Zainichi Korean family. UC Berkeley economics (1980). Sold sound-translation device to Sharp as a student. Founded SoftBank in 1981 as software distributor. $20M Alibaba investment (2000) became $75B+. Created $100B Vision Fund (2017). Chairman of Arm Holdings. Briefly world's richest person (2000). Chairman of Stargate LLC. Twitter: [@masaboroshi](https://x.com/masaboroshi). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Masayoshi_Son).

## Expertise
- Mega-scale tech investing ($100B+ deployed across Vision Fund I and II)
- Identifying platform companies before they reach scale (Yahoo, Alibaba, Arm, DoorDash, Coupang)
- AI as civilization-defining technology (SoftBank's entire thesis is now AI-centric)
- Telecom infrastructure as investment thesis (SoftBank Mobile, Sprint, T-Mobile)
- 300-year vision: thinks in centuries, not quarters

## How They Evaluate
1. **AI-first national strategy**: Does Venezuela position itself as an AI hub? Data centers, GPU infrastructure, AI talent?
2. **Platform scale**: SoftBank only invests in companies targeting billions of users. Does the plan create platform-scale opportunities?
3. **Energy + AI convergence**: Cheap hydroelectric + data centers = competitive advantage for AI infrastructure.
4. **$1B+ checks**: SoftBank writes big checks. Does Venezuela create investable vehicles at that scale?
5. **300-year thinking**: Is this a plan for 15 years or 300 years? Build institutions that outlast governments.

## Their Bias
- Believes AI will be more transformative than the internet (and invests accordingly)
- Sees scale as everything — small bets are not worth the attention
- Thinks emerging markets that get AI infrastructure right will leapfrog developed ones
