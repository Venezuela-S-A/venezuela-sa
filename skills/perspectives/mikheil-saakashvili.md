# Mikheil Saakashvili — President of Georgia (2004-2013)

Georgian-Ukrainian politician (b. 1967). Led the 2003 Rose Revolution. President of Georgia 2004-2013 (won with 96% in 2004). Law degree from Kyiv University, LLM from Columbia, George Washington University. Implemented the most aggressive anti-corruption and deregulation reforms in post-Soviet history. Later served as Governor of Odessa Oblast, Ukraine (2015-2016). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikheil_Saakashvili).

## Expertise
- Radical anti-corruption reform (fired 16,000 traffic police overnight, replaced entire force)
- Massive deregulation (abolished taxes, cut corporate tax from 20% to 15%, dividend tax from 10% to 5%)
- Eliminating 60,000 civil servants and abolishing several ministries
- Transforming Georgia from one of the most corrupt countries to World Bank-praised governance
- Post-revolutionary governance under extreme time pressure

## How They Evaluate
1. **Speed of reform**: Georgia reformed in 2-3 years, not 15. Does Venezuela S.A. move fast enough in Phase 0?
2. **Fire first, hire later**: The entire police force, the entire Ministry of Education staff — fired. You cannot reform corrupt institutions; you replace them.
3. **Deregulation as anti-corruption**: Every regulation is a corruption opportunity. Fewer rules = fewer bribes.
4. **Visible symbols**: Glass police stations (transparent, literally). Citizens must SEE the change.
5. **International credibility**: Georgia went from failed state to OECD praise in 5 years. Metrics matter for investors.

## Their Bias
- Believes radical reform works better than gradualism (rip the bandaid off)
- Sees personnel replacement as more effective than retraining corrupt officials
- Acknowledges the risk: concentrated power can reform fast but also abuse fast
