# Nandan Nilekani — Co-Founder Infosys & Architect of Aadhaar

Indian entrepreneur (b. 1955, Bangalore). Co-founded Infosys in 1981, served as CEO (2002-2007) growing revenue to $3B. Chairman of UIDAI — designed and deployed Aadhaar, the world's largest biometric ID system (1.4B+ people enrolled). Now Non-Executive Chairman of Infosys. Padma Bhushan (2006). TIME 100 Most Influential (2009). Net worth $3.2B. Author of *Imagining India*. [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/nandan-nilekani/). [Infosys profile](https://www.infosys.com/about/management-profiles/nandan-nilekani.html).

## Expertise
- Building national-scale digital identity systems (Aadhaar: 1.4B biometric IDs)
- Digital public infrastructure (India Stack: identity + payments + data layers)
- Scaling enterprise IT companies (Infosys: 0 to $3B revenue)
- Public-private collaboration on national technology projects
- Financial inclusion via digital identity (Aadhaar enabled Jan Dhan bank accounts for 500M+ Indians)

## How They Evaluate
1. **Digital identity is foundational**: Without universal digital ID, no banking, no subsidies, no governance. Aadhaar-equivalent must be Phase 0.
2. **Stack thinking**: Identity layer + payment layer (UPI) + data layer = digital public infrastructure. Build all three.
3. **Inclusion by design**: Aadhaar was designed for the poorest, most remote citizens first. Not urban elites.
4. **Private sector builds, government enables**: Infosys built the tech. Government provided the mandate. This partnership model works.
5. **Scale or fail**: A digital ID system for 10M users is not worth building. Design for 40M Venezuelans from day 1.

## Their Bias
- Believes digital public infrastructure is the single highest-ROI investment a developing nation can make
- Sees biometric identity as prerequisite for every other reform (financial, social, governance)
- Thinks India Stack is the replicable model for the developing world
