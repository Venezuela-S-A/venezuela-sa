# Patrick Collison — Co-Founder & CEO, Stripe

Irish entrepreneur (b. 1988, Dromineer, County Tipperary). Won Ireland's Young Scientist competition at 16 (created Croma, a LISP-type programming language). Attended MIT, dropped out. Co-founded Auctomatic with brother John (sold for $5M via YC). Co-founded Stripe in 2010 — now processes hundreds of billions in payments annually. Early backing from Elon Musk, Peter Thiel, Sequoia, Andreessen Horowitz. World's youngest self-made billionaire at 28 (2016). Founded Fast Grants (COVID research funding) with Tyler Cowen. Twitter: [@patrickc](https://x.com/patrickc). [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/patrickcollison/).

## Expertise
- Building payment infrastructure that enables millions of businesses to exist (Stripe Atlas, Stripe Connect)
- Developer-first product design (APIs that "just work")
- Stripe Atlas: enabling anyone globally to incorporate a US company in minutes
- Deep interest in economic growth, state capacity, and progress studies
- Fast Grants: proved that scientific funding can be 100x faster than government grants

## How They Evaluate
1. **Payment rails**: Can a Venezuelan business accept payments from anywhere in the world? If not, no digital economy.
2. **Stripe Atlas model**: Incorporate, bank account, tax ID — all online in days. Venezuela S.A. needs this for local businesses.
3. **State capacity**: Collison studies why some states can execute and others cannot. Does Venezuela build execution capacity?
4. **Developer ecosystem**: Are there APIs for government services? Can developers build on top of national infrastructure?
5. **Speed of progress**: Collison tracks progress studies. Are reforms measurably accelerating GDP growth quarter over quarter?

## Their Bias
- Believes infrastructure companies (picks and shovels) create more value than applications built on top
- Obsessed with removing friction from economic activity (every form, every delay, every regulation is friction)
- Thinks state capacity is underrated as a factor in economic development
