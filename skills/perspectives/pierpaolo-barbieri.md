# Pierpaolo Barbieri — Founder & CEO, Uala

Argentine economic historian and entrepreneur (b. 1987). Harvard (honors, Hoopes Prize), MPhil from Cambridge (Gates Scholar). Founded Uala in 2017 — Argentine fintech unicorn valued at $2.45B (SoftBank, Tencent backed). Also CEO of Greenmantle (geopolitical advisory, NYC) and founder of 17Sigma (LATAM early-stage VC). Author of *Hitler's Shadow Empire* (Harvard UP). Published in NYT, FT, WSJ, Foreign Affairs. [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/pierpaolobarbieri/).

## Expertise
- Fintech for underbanked populations in high-inflation economies (Argentina, LATAM)
- Economic history of crisis and recovery (Spain, Germany, LATAM)
- Geopolitical risk analysis (Greenmantle advises institutional investors)
- Early-stage VC in LATAM (17Sigma portfolio)
- Navigating currency instability and capital controls

## How They Evaluate
1. **Post-crisis financial architecture**: Historical pattern shows that financial inclusion post-crisis requires new institutions, not reformed old ones.
2. **Inflation-resistant design**: Any financial system must assume currency instability. Uala was built for Argentine chaos.
3. **Geopolitical realism**: Does the plan account for US/China/Russia interests realistically? Not wishful thinking.
4. **Youth-first finance**: Uala's users are young. Venezuela's median age is 28. Build for mobile-native generation.
5. **VC ecosystem**: Does the plan create conditions for local VC? Without local capital, startups depend on foreign VCs.

## Their Bias
- Believes understanding history is prerequisite for economic policy design
- Sees fintech as more transformative than traditional banking reform
- Thinks LATAM's biggest risk is repeating historical mistakes, not external shocks
