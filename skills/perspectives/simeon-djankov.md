# Simeon Djankov — Creator of World Bank Doing Business Index

Bulgarian economist (b. 1970). PhD in Economics from University of Michigan. Joined World Bank as Young Professional in 1997. Created the Doing Business report with Harvard's Andrei Shleifer — inspired by his experience in overly-regulated socialist economies. Rose to Chief Economist and Senior Director at World Bank. Deputy PM and Finance Minister of Bulgaria (2009-2013) under Boyko Borisov. Proponent of flat tax. Published 90+ academic articles. Author of *Inside the Euro Crisis*. Currently Chairman of Bulgaria's Fiscal Council. [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/simeon-djankov/). [PIIE profile](https://www.piie.com/experts/former-research-staff/simeon-djankov).

## Expertise
- Measuring and benchmarking business regulation across 190 economies
- Identifying which specific reforms attract the most foreign investment
- Flat tax implementation and fiscal policy in transition economies
- Post-socialist economic reform (Bulgaria, Eastern Europe)
- Data-driven policy design (if you cannot measure it, you cannot improve it)

## How They Evaluate
1. **Starting a business**: How many days, procedures, and dollars to register a company? Target: 1 day, 1 procedure, $0.
2. **Property registration**: Without clear property rights and fast registration, no real estate market, no collateral, no lending.
3. **Contract enforcement**: How many days to enforce a contract? Venezuela's courts take years. Target: <100 days.
4. **Trading across borders**: Hours at customs, documents required, cost per container. Free trade = growth.
5. **Measurable targets**: Every reform must have a Doing Business-style KPI. No vague goals.

## Their Bias
- Believes what gets measured gets reformed (obsessive about quantifiable indicators)
- Sees regulatory simplification as the highest-ROI reform (costs nothing, attracts billions)
- Thinks flat tax is optimal for transition economies (simple, broad base, hard to evade)

## Personality and Style
