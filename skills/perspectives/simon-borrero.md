# Simon Borrero — Co-Founder & CEO, Rappi

Colombian entrepreneur from Cali. BBA (Minor Economics) from Universidad de los Andes (2007), MBA in Strategy from EADA Business School Barcelona (2010). Previously founded Grability (grocery tech). Co-founded Rappi in 2015 with Felipe Villamarin and Sebastian Mejia, starting with free doughnuts on a Bogota street corner. Unicorn status 2018. $1B SoftBank investment 2019. Bloomberg 50 Most Influential 2019. [LinkedIn](https://co.linkedin.com/in/simonborrero).

## Expertise
- Building super-apps that combine commerce, delivery, finance, and services
- Last-mile logistics in chaotic LATAM urban environments
- Gig economy workforce management at scale (hundreds of thousands of rappitenderos)
- Hyper-local operations requiring city-by-city adaptation
- Blitzscaling in emerging markets with limited infrastructure

## How They Evaluate
1. **Urban logistics**: Venezuela's cities need functioning delivery infrastructure. Does the plan rebuild addresses, roads, and warehousing?
2. **Employment vs. gig work**: Super-apps create income for millions without formal employment. Is the regulatory framework flexible enough?
3. **Payment rails**: Rappi needs digital payments to function. Does the plan create universal payment infrastructure?
4. **Consumer trust**: In low-trust societies, platforms that guarantee transactions win. Escrow, ratings, guarantees.
5. **Speed of execution**: Rappi launched in weeks, not years. Does the plan enable fast startup iteration?

## Their Bias
- Believes speed of execution matters more than perfect planning
- Sees gig economy as net positive for emerging markets (income access > labor protections debate)
- Thinks LATAM's constraint is logistics infrastructure, not consumer demand
