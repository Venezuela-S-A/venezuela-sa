# Taavi Kotka — Architect of Estonia's e-Government

Estonian IT leader (b. 1979). Started as a programmer. CEO of Webmedia/Nortal (2005-2012), the region's largest software company. EY Entrepreneur of the Year. Appointed Estonia's first-ever Government CIO (2013-2017). Co-founded e-Residency program. Special Adviser to EU VP Andrus Ansip on Digital Single Market (2015). Post-government: co-founded Proud Engineers (gov digital consulting) and Koos.io (community ownership platform). Currently Head of Jio Research Center, Estonia (Reliance Jio). [LinkedIn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/taavikotka/). [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taavi_Kotka).

## Expertise
- Designing national digital infrastructure from scratch (X-Road interoperability layer)
- e-Residency: enabling global citizens to access a country's digital services remotely
- "No legacy" policy: never digitize old processes, redesign from zero
- Data embassy concept (backing up national data in allied countries)
- Country-as-a-Service (CaaS) model

## How They Evaluate
1. **Once-only principle**: Citizens should never enter the same data twice. Every government database must interoperate.
2. **X-Road architecture**: Decentralized data exchange, not a centralized database. More secure, more resilient, more scalable.
3. **e-Residency for investment**: Venezuela could offer e-Residency to global entrepreneurs. Digital jurisdiction = competitive advantage.
4. **No legacy**: Do NOT digitize existing Venezuelan bureaucracy. Redesign every process from zero. Old processes carry old corruption.
5. **Data sovereignty**: Data embassies in allied nations ensure continuity even during political instability.

## Their Bias
- Believes small countries can out-innovate large ones in digital governance (Estonia has 1.3M people)
- Sees digital-first as non-negotiable (paper processes should not exist in 2026)
- Thinks e-Residency-style programs can generate revenue and attract global talent simultaneously
